Just a few decades ago, there were almost no wolves left in the United States.

They had been systematically hunted, trapped and poisoned to near extinction.

In the mid-1990's the federal government reintroduced wolves to the Northern Rockies - transplanting 76 animals from Canada to Yellowstone and Central Idaho.

Their population boomed and a culture war erupted.

If you've ever seen a wolf, even just on tv, the chances are it was through a long distance scope. But that doesn’t make seeing one in the wild any less magical. Most of the wolves in Yellowstone have been tagged and collared and enthusiasts monitor their whereabouts and alert each other when there’s a possible wolf sighting.

Something else about how wolves are really popular, facebook pages, etc.

Doug Smith, a biologist and head of the Yellowstone Wolf Project has heard the reintroduction of wolves to the Yellowstone area and Central Idaho as America’s most significant wildlife conservation accomplishment in the 20th Century. A whole wolf watching industry has blossomed along with the wolves population.
